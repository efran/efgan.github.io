Introduction

James is a rising senior undergraduate student from Seattle, WA with extensive management and marketing experience, with a focus in technology.

James is particularly interested in technology and management. He hopes to work in the technology industry or in a role involving technology with the largest social impact possible - that is why he is applying to the Cornell Tech MBA, to gain the skills and connections necessary to make a difference.

James pursues new experiences and knowledge during his free time. He has lived in four countries, and speaks four languages. He volunteers extensively, having led a nonprofit's regional operations to help promote STEM education and close education gaps.

Creativity at Cornell

Currently pursuing a B.A. in Economics at Cornell University, with minors in Computer Science and Information Science.

As a Cornell undergraduate, James has participated in numerous hackathons including BigRedHacks, the Hilton Hospitality Hackathon, and DubHacks. Highlights of these hackathons are detailed in the section below.
As the Treasurer for Mixed, James created events and materials to foster a community for mixed race students.

During his junior year of undergraduate studies, James studied abroad at the University of New South Wales in Sydney, Australia. While in Sydney, he was a semifinalist for the Peter Farrell Cup, a business competition, as the Chief Product Officer for a company which focused on connecting designers with manufacturers through an interactive online bidding and licensing system which leveraged 3D displays of designs.
